Experience

Die Engineer

A die engineer works on the entire life cycle of die for a company. My responsibilities are establishing the development process and the manufacturing parameters for die components, troubleshooting quality issues, making corrective measures, guiding designers to create castings, documenting production cost, and maintaining the casting equipment. My duties also include working with the tooling engineer to build slide layouts, internal cooling systems, air evacuation systems, and parting lines for the die.

Mold Engineer

Using CAD software to create plastic molds for manufacturing processes such as injection molding. The position entails designing molds using computer design software such as AutoCAD, Unigraphics, Pro-Engineer (Pro-E), and Solidworks. Once the molds are created the designer should visit the manufacturing facilities to participate in the mold trials to ensure they produce parts and products as designed. I also have experience with understanding the intricacies of making molds for plastics and have expert skills on how the injection molding process works.

Tooling Engineer

Using CAD manufacturing to create and adjust tools or parts for vehicles, airplanes, heavy equipment, or any product that needs customized components. As a tooling engineer, my duties include drafting blueprints for designs and crafting the necessary pieces. I also improve previous designs. I have experience with metalworking and mechanics. Computer skills are just also important because tooling engineers build and troubleshoot designs with CAD software.
